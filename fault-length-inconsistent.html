Why Is My Wire Cutting Length Inconsistent?

Unstable cutting length usually comes from feeding slip, tension variation, encoder problems, or parameter storage issues.

Problem Description

The machine may be set to one length, but finished wires come out short, long, or different from piece to piece. This can affect downstream crimping, assembly, and final harness quality.

Common Causes and Solutions

01Feeding roller pressure uneven

→ Fix: Adjust the roller pressure evenly on both sides and confirm the wire feeds smoothly without slipping.

02Wire spool tension too high or too low

→ Fix: Use a smoother payoff direction and keep wire tension stable before it enters the machine.

03Length encoder dirty or slipping

→ Fix: Clean the encoder wheel or measuring roller and check whether it rotates consistently with wire movement.

04Parameter not saved after adjustment

→ Fix: Re-enter the length value, save the program correctly, then restart and confirm the setting remains.

How to Choose the Right Stripping Blade for Your Wire

The correct blade helps protect copper strands, improve stripping consistency, and reduce scrap in daily production.

Why Blade Selection Matters

Wire insulation, conductor size, and jacket hardness all affect stripping quality. A mismatched blade can cut copper, leave insulation residue, or make stripping length unstable.

Blade Rules by Wire Size

Fine wire usually needs precise blade opening and light pressure. Standard electronic wire can use common V-type or matching machine blades. Heavy wire may need stronger blade material and slower cutting action.

Common Blade Mistakes

If the blade is set too deep, copper strands may be damaged. If it is too shallow, insulation may not separate cleanly and operators may pull the jacket by hand.

How to Send Wire Samples for Confirmation

Send a clear wire photo, conductor size, insulation material, target stripping length, and sample stripping result. A short machine video also helps confirm blade depth and guide alignment.

Need blade model confirmation?

Send your wire sample photo and machine model. We can help check blade type before you order parts.

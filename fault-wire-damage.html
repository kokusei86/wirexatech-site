Why Is My Wire Getting Damaged During Stripping?

Wire damage usually means the blade, guide path, or processing speed is not matched to the wire material.

Problem Description

During stripping, the insulation may tear badly, copper strands may be nicked, or the wire end may bend and deform. If this continues in batch production, it can cause unstable electrical performance and high scrap rate.

Common Causes and Solutions

01Blade cutting too deep

→ Fix: Reduce blade depth step by step until insulation separates cleanly without touching copper strands.

02Blade worn or chipped

→ Fix: Inspect the blade edge under good light. Replace chipped or dull blades before continuing production.

03Wire not aligned in guide tube

→ Fix: Choose the correct guide tube size and align the inlet, rollers, blade center, and outlet path.

04Stripping speed too fast for wire material

→ Fix: Reduce processing speed for soft insulation, fine wire, or difficult jacket material, then test several samples.
